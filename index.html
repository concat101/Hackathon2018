Rescue Wolverine


Earthquakes are one of the deadliest natural disasters in the world that can cause thousands of deaths and millions homeless in an instance. Unfortunately, some countries do not have a proper disaster mitigation plan in place when an earthquake strikes. Often, victims get trapped inside buildings and do not get help in time.

We here at Concat101 has come-up with a rescue robot that can detect movement and send a response to the website. Wolverine, the rescue robot is remote controlled and will be placed in areas that a person will not be able to enter and detect movement within a 3m to 5m radius.

Wolverine is equipped with a PIR motion sensor that has an operating voltage range of 4.5 to 20V and a lens size sensor of 0.23mm in diameter. The board dimensions is 32mm by 24mm and can be placed on top of a small remote controlled car.

The technologies used for this project are: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, C#, C++, ASP.NET, Bootstrap, JSON, and Particle Electron Kit.

Motion Sensor

HC-SR501 uses an infrared technology that is based on a Germany imported LHI778 design. It has a sensing range of less than 120 degrees and covers a radius of 7 meters

All-weather terrain

The material of the prototype model will offer protection against dust, shock, vibration, and blowing rain. It is not meant for underwater however, it can be immersed for up to 20 minutes.

Battery

We use a Lithium-ion (Li-ion) battery based on 2 factors: weight and energy capacity. The Li-ion batteries are lighter than your standard Alkaline batteries and has a high energy density.

Camera

The camera will be implemented in future model pending the success of our current model. It will be equipped with night vision for monitoring in the dark and will transmit data via cellular data.

Detecting....

0 = static 1 = incoming signal

The PIR sensor is initially static and monitors for any signal within a 3-meter radius. Once a signal is picked up, it will be delayed for 5 seconds and will send the signal to the cloud using cellular data. The signal will then be uploaded to the laptop and be converted into readable data. If the curve goes up, it indicates there is movement in the sensor. If it rests, there is no movement. The graph is real-time as it collects data every second
